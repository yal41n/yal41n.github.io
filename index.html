FAQ

Frequently asked questions

The hackathon is open to anyone 18 years or older — developers, security engineers, data scientists, designers, and students. You don't need to be a cybersecurity expert; teams with diverse skills tend to perform best.
Teams must have between 2 and 5 members. Solo participants are not eligible. If you don't have a team yet, join the #team-formation channel on our Discord to connect with others looking to team up.
Yes — you may use open-source libraries, frameworks, and pre-trained models. However, the core innovation and implementation must be created during the hackathon. Clearly disclose all third-party components in your submission.
CyberGAP Hackathon 2025 is hybrid. Remote participation is fully supported with an online platform, virtual mentor sessions, and live-streamed awards. In-person hubs will be available in New York, London, and Singapore.
All submissions are evaluated by a panel of industry experts on four criteria: Innovation (30%), Security Impact (30%), Technical Depth (25%), and Presentation Quality (15%). The top 10 teams per track are invited for a live pitch session before final rankings are determined.
You retain full ownership of your intellectual property. By submitting, you grant CyberGAP a non-exclusive license to showcase your project in our portfolio and communications. No rights are transferred to sponsors unless you enter into a separate agreement.
